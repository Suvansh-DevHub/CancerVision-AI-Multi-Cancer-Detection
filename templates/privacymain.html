1. Introduction

CancerVision ("we," "us," "our") is an AI-driven medical imaging platform designed to analyze CT, X-ray, MRI, mammography, and dermoscopy scans for cancer detection and subtype classification.

This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, process, store, and protect your information when you access our platform or any related services, applications, or websites.

Please review this policy carefully. If you do not agree with any part of our practices, you should not use CancerVision.

Privacy Overview

Last Updated: November 2025 | Effective Date: December 1, 2025

We handle medical imaging data and personal health information with strict confidentiality and compliance standards. All data is encrypted, securely stored, and processed solely for diagnostic evaluation and platform functionality. We do not sell or share your data for marketing purposes. Our practices align with HIPAA, GDPR, and other applicable medical data protection regulations.

3. DICOM & Medical Image Processing

What is DICOM?

DICOM (Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine) is the international standard for storing and transmitting medical images. CancerVision receives DICOM files containing medical images and associated metadata such as patient demographics, study parameters, acquisition parameters, and imaging protocols.

How We Process Medical Images:

Step Process Data Handling
1. Upload & Ingestion DICOM files received securely via encrypted channels (TLS 1.3+) Immediate encryption at rest; virus scans performed
2. Anonymization (Optional) Removal of PII from DICOM headers per user preference Original data retained separately for clinical correlation
3. Preprocessing Normalization, windowing, and format conversion
  • Automatic slice selection based on modality standards
  • Artifact and noise detection during preprocessing
  • Intensity, contrast, and geometric normalization
 No data retention; temporary processing only
4. AI Inference Multi-organ analysis using trained neural networks
  • Multi-stage prediction pipeline across anatomical regions
  • Confidence scores and uncertainty estimates calculated
  • Heatmaps and segmentation masks generated for interpretability
 Processed on isolated GPU clusters with access logging
5. Result Generation Probability scores, segmentation masks, heatmaps; structured clinical summaries Results encrypted and linked to original study

Modality-Aware Processing:

  • CT/X-ray: Windowing parameters applied; bone and soft-tissue regions enhanced
  • MRI: Sequence-specific processing (T1, T2, FLAIR) and signal normalization
  • Mammography: Density analysis and region-of-interest extraction
  • Dermoscopy: Color normalization and texture analysis

Pixel-Level Data Considerations:

Medical images inherently contain sensitive information beyond text metadata. Even anonymized DICOM images can theoretically be re-identified. CancerVision treats all pixel data as PHI and applies the same security controls as text-based medical records.

4. AI Inference & Explainability (Grad-CAM)

Our AI Models:

CancerVision uses deep learning models trained on large datasets to detect and classify abnormalities in medical images. Our models are specialized for:

  • Lung cancer detection in chest CT scans
  • Liver lesion classification in abdominal imaging
  • Breast density and suspicious findings in mammography
  • Brain abnormality localization in MRI studies
  • Bone and musculoskeletal pathology detection

Explainability via Grad-CAM:

Grad-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping) is a technique we use to make AI predictions interpretable to radiologists. Here's what happens with your data:

How Grad-CAM Works:
1. The AI model processes your medical image and generates predictions (e.g., "suspicious lesion detected with 87% confidence").
2. We compute gradient information from the model's decision layer.
3. These gradients are mapped back to the image space, creating a heatmap highlighting regions that influenced the AI prediction.
4. The heatmap is overlaid on the original image, showing radiologists exactly which anatomical areas the model considered.
5. This heatmap and confidence scores are provided as part of the analysis report.

Data Usage in AI Processing:

  • Real-time inference: Your images are processed on-demand; not stored permanently in model memory
  • Model improvement: With your explicit consent, anonymized images may be used to retrain and improve model performance
  • Audit trails: All AI inferences are logged with timestamps, model versions, and operator IDs for regulatory compliance
  • No external sharing: Models are not shared with third parties; inference is performed exclusively on our infrastructure
⚠️ Important: AI-generated results are diagnostic aids only. All reports must be reviewed and approved by a licensed radiologist before clinical use. CancerVision does not provide autonomous diagnosis.

5. Data Storage & Retention Policies

Where Data is Stored:

  • Geographic location: Primary storage in HIPAA-compliant data centers (regionally deployed for local regulatory requirements)
  • Redundancy: Data replicated across multiple availability zones for disaster recovery
  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit
  • Access control: Role-based access; all access logged and monitored

Retention Schedules:

Data Type Retention Period Rationale
Medical Images (DICOM) 7 years (unless clinically indicated longer) Compliance with applicable medical record retention laws and clinical requirements
Analysis Results & Reports 7 years Medical record retention requirements
Personal Account Data Until account deletion or inactivity (6 years) Service continuity and regulatory hold periods
Access Logs & Audit Trails 3 years minimum Compliance audit and forensic investigation
Session Cookies & Temporary Cache Until end of session or 30 days Session management and performance optimization

Default Processing Window:

By default, uploaded images and analysis results are retained in our secure processing environment for 30 days. After this period, data is automatically deleted unless you create a saved account or explicitly choose to retain the data.

Saved Account Storage:

If you create a CancerVision account and opt to save analyses:

  • Images and reports may be stored until you delete them.
  • You retain full control and may delete any saved analysis at any time.
  • We recommend periodic review and deletion of historical analyses where appropriate.

Anonymized Model Training (Opt-In):

Separately from service retention, we offer an optional program to contribute anonymized image data to improve our AI models:

  • Only images with explicit opt-in consent are included.
  • All personally identifiable information and sensitive DICOM headers are removed prior to inclusion.
  • Anonymized images used for model training are stored in a separate, secure training repository.
  • You may withdraw consent at any time; data already used in model training cannot be retroactively removed from trained models.

Backup & Disaster Recovery:

We maintain encrypted backups in geographically distributed data centers for up to 90 days to ensure service continuity and disaster recovery. Backup copies are retained only for the minimum period necessary to restore service and are purged thereafter.

Deletion & Purging:

Upon request or at the end of the retention period, data is securely purged using cryptographic erasure (DEK destruction) or physical destruction of storage media. Backups are similarly purged after 90 days following the primary deletion date.

  • Prompt notification of any security incidents or breaches

  • Explicit user consent is required for research participation

No Data Sales:

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or medical images to marketing firms, data brokers, advertisers, or any third party for commercial gain. Your medical data remains confidential at all times.

7. Security Measures

Technical Safeguards:

  • Encryption: AES-256 at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit; end-to-end encryption for sensitive communications
  • Authentication: Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all user accounts; OAuth 2.0 / OpenID Connect for integrations
  • Network security: Web Application Firewall (WAF), DDoS protection, intrusion detection system (IDS)
  • Database security: Secrets management (HashiCorp Vault); encryption keys stored in Hardware Security Modules (HSM)
  • Isolation: Images processed on air-gapped, isolated GPU clusters with strict access controls; no internet access from compute nodes
  • Data integrity: Checksums and cryptographic hashing verify image and report integrity
  • Audit logging: Detailed audit logs record access and modifications to medical data for forensic and compliance purposes
  • Infrastructure maintenance: Servers are patched and updated automatically to reduce vulnerability exposure
  • Physical security: Physical data centers employ multi-factor entry controls and biometric access where available
  • SOC monitoring: Dedicated Security Operations Center (SOC) monitors security telemetry 24/7

Administrative Safeguards:

  • Role-based access control (RBAC) with principle of least privilege
  • Employee background checks, NDA agreements, and HIPAA training (annual)
  • Incident response plan with defined escalation procedures and 72-hour breach notification protocol
  • Business continuity and disaster recovery processes are in place to maintain service availability and support recovery
  • Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments

Compliance & Certifications:

CancerVision maintains the following security certifications:

  • HIPAA Compliance (Business Associate Agreement available)
  • GDPR Compliance (Data Controller & Processor roles defined)
  • SOC 2 Type II certification
  • ISO 27001 (Information Security Management)
  • FDA 21 CFR Part 11 compliance (for regulated submissions)
Security Incident Reporting: If you suspect unauthorized access or a data breach, contact our security team immediately at security@cancervision.com. We will investigate and notify affected parties within 72 hours as required by applicable law.

8. Your Privacy Rights

Right to Access (GDPR Article 15 / HIPAA):

You have the right to request a copy of all personal and medical data we hold about you in a portable, machine-readable format (DICOM files, CSV exports). Requests will be fulfilled within 30 days.

Right to Correct (GDPR Article 16 / HIPAA):

You can request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data. For medical records, corrections are flagged and original data retained for audit purposes per HIPAA standards.

Right to Deletion (GDPR Article 17 - "Right to be Forgotten"):

Subject to legal and regulatory holds, you may request deletion of your data. Note: Deletion of medical images is typically not permitted during the retention period unless justified by clinical necessity.

Right to Data Portability (GDPR Article 20):

You can request all your data in a standard, transferable format to move to another provider or system.

Right to Withdraw Consent:

If we process data based on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time. Withdrawal does not affect data processed before withdrawal.

Right to Object (GDPR Article 21):

You can object to specific processing activities (e.g., research use of anonymized data) and we will cease that processing where legally permissible.

Right to Restrict Processing (GDPR Article 18):

You may request that we temporarily limit how your data is processed while you verify accuracy, raise an objection, or pursue a complaint.

Right to Lodge a Complaint:

You may file a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe your privacy rights have been violated. You may also contact us for assistance in understanding your rights.

How to Exercise Your Rights:

Submit a written request to our Data Protection Officer at dpo@cancervision.com with proof of identity (driver's license, insurance card). All requests will be processed within 30 days (GDPR) or per applicable state laws.

10. Children's Privacy

CancerVision is designed for medical professionals and is not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, we will immediately delete such data.

For Patients Aged 13-17:

In jurisdictions that require additional protections for minors, parental or guardian consent is mandatory before uploading pediatric imaging studies. Healthcare providers are responsible for obtaining and documenting this authorization.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided information to CancerVision, please contact us immediately at support@cancervision.com.
